LifeOS Features base list

1. Core functions (The Basics)

⦁	Hands-Free Calls: Make, receive, and manage phone calls.
⦁	Media Playback: Listen to music, podcasts, and audiobooks with open-ear speakers.
⦁	Notifications Reader: The AI discreetly whispers your incoming texts, emails, and app alerts.
⦁	Voice Commands: Standard assistant tasks (e.g., "Send a text to Mom," "What's the weather?," "Add milk to my shopping list").
⦁	Timers & Reminders: Set alarms, timers, and calendar event reminders.
⦁	Quick Capture: Instantly take a photo or record a video (from your first-person perspective) with a voice command or tap, saving it to your phone.
⦁	Basic Q&A: Ask general knowledge questions ("Who won the game last night?").


2. Real-World Interaction

⦁	Live Text Reader: You look at a menu, book, or sign and ask, "Read this to me."
⦁	Commerce: "Tell me about this product." (Get reviews, prices, or nutritional info).
⦁	Nature: "What kind of dog/plant is that?"
⦁	Landmark & Location ID: "What building am I looking at?" (Get name, history, and hours).


3. Advanced and Doable Save and Tell

⦁	"Remember This": A "bookmark" for your life. You say, "Remember this" while looking at a pill bottle, a door code, or a person, and the AI captures the visual and audio context.
⦁	Descriptive Recall: You ask, "Where did I leave my keys?" and the AI describes the memory: "They are on your kitchen counter, next to the coffee maker."
⦁	Social Recall (with permission): You discreetly ask, "Who am I talking to?" and the AI whispers, "This is Sarah; you met her last month. You discussed hiking."

4. Advanced Features

⦁	Audio-Only Navigation: The AI provides turn-by-turn walking directions ("Turn right at the next corner, by the blue mailbox").
⦁	Live Transcription: Create a text document from a lecture or meeting in real-time.

5. The Damn features

⦁	Brainstorming Catalyst: You're stuck in front of a whiteboard. The AI uses your environment for prompts: "I see a coffee cup and a plant. What if we connect your idea to 'energy' or 'growth'?"
⦁	The Serendipity Engine: You tell the AI you're interested in "art." As you walk around, it proactively whispers interesting, non-obvious facts about architecture or street art you happen to look at.
